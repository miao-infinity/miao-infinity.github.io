About

I'm a student researcher in Robotics with a soft spot for Reinforcement Learning. After earning my M.Sc. in Dynamic Systems & Controls at Boston University, I became a Ph.D. student in Robotics at Stevens Institute of Technology working on navigation and manipulation problems for safety-critical applications.

I'm interested in developing algorithms that enable robots to learn faster, adapt more efficiently, and perform complex tasks in real-world environments. I enjoy exploring the intersection of reinforcement learning, multi-agent systems, and control theory.

Beyond research, I enjoy activities that challenge both body and mind. I climb (currently V7 bouldering / 5.12 sport), and I previously competed as a table tennis athlete representing the Shanxi provincial team in China.

My name '淼' means 'boundlessness of the ocean' in Chinese, pronounced: 'meow'.
